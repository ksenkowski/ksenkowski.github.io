Imperial Guardsman

The Imperial Guard was originally formed as nine Omada (500 men), with three kept on duty in the capital while the six others served the Emperador's will at various hot spots around the Empire. During these early days of the Empire they remained a tool of the Emperadors with little political ambition. This however changed during the reign of Tiberio. They remained loyal and established themselves as a semi-independent political force.

During the civil war at the end of the Second Era, the Guard sided against Cid and was destroyed at the battle of Muerlenta Bridge. They remained disbanded until Vasilikos II hired a small army of Navari mercenaries from his brother-in-law Vladimir of Bravich to defend his throne. He divided the survivors into nine Omada as had existed at the dawn of the Empire, dividing their duties between military service and working as the Emperador's personal intelligence service.

Talents
  • Cult of the Emperador
    Requirements: None
    All Imperial Guardsmen are trained to view combat as a religious experience. The draw and strike is the perfect Moment Between Moments, which is where the divine is found. Add 2D to the first strike when the character has Tempo at the start of a Skirmish.
  • Field Training
    Requirements: None
    All Imperial Guardsmen get some level of medical field training to close wounds and deal with common illnesses. Those whom engage in advanced training become so adept at this that seem to be able to cure almost any ailment with ease. Add +1R to attempts to heal yourself or others.
  • Elite of the Elite
    Requirements: Cult of the Emperador
    The toughest of the Imperial Guard join a training program run high in the mountains north of the Great City at the Monastery of Cid. There they spend 6 months engaging in a violently difficult program of which only 1% graduate. +3 to Vitality Pool total. +1 to Vitality Recovery. +1R to survival based Challenges
  • Stratios
    Requirements: None
    The Imperial Guard is split among two branches, the military and the intelligence service. The Stratios is the lowest level of training a member of the military arm receives, involving both combat training and educational doctrine. +1R to Challenges that involve knowledge of the Imperial Guard, the History of the Empire and the Imperial Court. +1R to Skirmish rolls when fighting with other Guardsmen.
  • Emperador's Eyes
    Requirements: None
    The backbone of the Imperial Guard intelligence service are the Emperador's Eyes, individuals trained in the observation and infiltration of the Emperador's enemies. It is their reports that keep the Imperial Guard one step ahead of everyone. Spend 3 Vitality to be at the right place to spy on a target. +1R to remain unnoticed in a crowd.
  • Emperador's Ears
    Requirements: Emperador's Eyes
    Some members of the Imperial Guard intelligence service are adept at putting together the puzzle that is created from an endless flow of information from the Eyes. Many plots have been stopped before they could truly begin because an Ear was able to piece together disparite bits of information. Spend 2 Influence to define one detail regarding a particular enemy's plans.
  • Emperador's Hands
    Requirements: Emperador's Eyes
    Not all actions of the Imperial Guard intelligence service are passive. Sometimes a squad from the watch or the military are a too overt solution to a problem. That is where the Hands come into play. Add +1R to clandestinely eliminate a threat to the Emperador.
  • Cavalry Man
    Requirements: Stratios
    The military side of the Imperial Guard employs a single Omada of horsemen. They are used as an elite strike force sent to delay approaching enemies or in some cases to shatter angry mobs. Increase weapon damage by 4 when fighting from horseback.
  • Lokias
    Requirements: Stratios
    Some members of the Imperial Guard are born leaders, and the organization is adept at identifying these individuals and putting them in positions to succeed. They are granted responsiblities above and beyond most members of their rank, allowing them the priveledge to call on resources without having to submit formal requests. Spend 3 Influence to call on 2-12 Guardsmen as allies when your location has an Imperial Garrison. Add +2 to Influence Pool total
  • Shattering Confidence
    Requirements: Cult of the Emperador
    The reputation of the Imperial Guard is such that even the deadliest foes are sometimes intimidated into making foolish mistakes. The soldiers of the Kalifate call them the Simal Qezeb, The Wrath of the North. Once per Skirmish you can spend 2 Willpower to force an enemy to hesitate, allowing you to take the Tempo.
Cultural Synergies

  • Beserker
    Requirements: Navari Culture
    The recent history of the Imperial Guard is one that is heavily influenced by the ways of the Navari. One such carry over is the allowance of religious zeal to overcome a warrior in battle, giving them a nearly endless supply of strength and endurance. Add +3 to Vitality Recovery.
  • Religious Conviction
    Requirements: Hellas Culture
    The intense training in the Cult of the Emperador can sometimes lead Hellas Imperial Guardsmen towards a deep and abiding conviction in the idea of the Moment Between Moments. When this happens, the Guardsman becomes almost unconscious in their combat abilities. At the start of a Skirmish you may spend 1 Willpower for an advantage die up to a total equal to the character's current Willpower Pool. These dice can be used as advantage dice for Melee Attacks, but once used are gone.
