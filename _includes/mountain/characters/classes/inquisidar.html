Inquisidar

Next to the Emperador, the Patriarcha of the Orthodox Church is the most powerful man in the Empire. From the ancient city of Corriente he he leads the war against other faiths and the darkness at the edges of the world. His soldiers in this war are the Inquisidars.

The Inquisidars make up the backbone of the Orthodox Church, serving as conduits to Vojin the All Father, political advisors, educators, hounds of heresy, and soldiers. They simultanesouly live both within society and outside of it as they ultimately answer to Mother Church and no one else.

Talents
  • Divine Authority
    Requirements: Esoteric Training
    The Church is the accepted religious authority in the Empire. True believers readily defer to the officers of the Church when they invoke the authority of the All Father and the Church. Add +1R in social Challenges with followers of the Orthodox Church.
  • Investigative Training
    Requirements: None
    Some Inquisidars hone their skills of observation and logical thinking to a sharp edge. They are further trained and put in the field as investigators, often coming into conflict with members of the criminal underworld. Add +1R to Challenges tied to an investigation and +2 to the character's Influence Pool.
  • Diabolisto
    Requirements: Esoteric Training
    The education of the Inquisidar can lead down dark, forbidden roads found only in the dark corners of the world. Texts banned yet not destroyed by the Church lie on black shelves, educating the poor fools who seek them out. Add +1R for Ritual Challenges. Define a Taint Aura and roll once on the Taint Effects Table.
  • Esoteric Training
    Requirements: None
    Nearly all Inquisidars go through a course of intense religious training to prepare them for a life walking the line between the secular and religious world. Add +1R to Challenges centered on matters concering the Church.
  • Great Orator
    Requirements: None
    The Church knows better than most the power of words backed by conviction and divine authority. Many Inquisidars master this power, using it to move men to action or to inaction. Add +2R to drive a crowd to action or inaction.
  • Descubridor
    Requirements: None
    Study is a key component to a Inquisidar's life in the Church regardless of the path they have taken. It is so important that many Inquisidar's hone the skill to the point where there is little they cannot learn from time spent reading in the library or asking questions of the faithful. Add +2R to discover the truth about a particular matter tied to the Church.
  • College Militargo
    Requirements: None
    Some Inquisidars choose to further their education in the Church sanctioned school of arms, the College Militargo. At the College, Inquisidars learn the tactics and training methods of the great leaders of antiquity so that they may better serve the defenders of the Church. They provide a +1R to all rolls to their side of a Skirmish for the first round.
  • Militargo Ordenado
    Requirements: College Militargo
    A Militargo Ordenado is charged with providing military support to secular lords which the Church owes temporal fealty to. They are effectively a captain of a contigent of lay soliders that have sworn an oath to the Church. Add +2 to your Influence Pool. The character may spend 2 Influence to summon a squad of 4 soliders and 1 seargant once per Turn if the Church has a garrison at the location. These characters can be added to your Network.
  • College Temporal
    Requirements: None
    Some Inquisidars believe that the best way to serve the Church is to work closely with secular authorties. At the College Temporal, Inquisidars are educated in the finer points of estate administation, secular law, and diplomacy. Add +1R to Challenges involving estate administration, secular law, and diplomacy.
  • Empailo
    Requirements: College Temporal
    One of the more difficult tasks of the Emperador is to appoint unbiased judges to administer secular justice in the Empire. The Empailo often fill this role, working a circuit administering Imperial justice. Add +4 Influence Pool. The character may pay 2 Influence to summon an Imperial Watch patrol (1 sgt and 4 soldiers) once per Turn if an Imperial Garrison is present.
Cultural Synergies

  • Church Service
    Requirements: Vor Culture
    Youngest sons in Vorish noble familys are often sent to serve the Church in a lay capacity. Those who show an affinity for the divine mysteries will often take holy orders, providing their family with powerful benifactor, at the cost of siring children. Add +4 Influence Pool and add +1R to Challenges regarding the Orthodox Church.
  • Family Heirloom
    Requirements: Hellas Culture
    It is not uncommon for a Hellas family to have a religious artifact in their possession that is as old if not older than the Empire itself. These famous items are believed to hold tremendous power, which often makes them targets for theft and even destruction. You may create an Artifact with up to a 4 Difficulty.
