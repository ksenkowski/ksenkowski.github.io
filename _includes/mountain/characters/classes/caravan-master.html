Caravan Master

Caravan Masters are the connective tissue of society, tying cultures separated by vast distances together. The lands they traverse are harsh and unforgiving, plagued by bandits and the miscommunication born of cultural differences. The very best Caravan Masters are master communicators with pretanatural senses for danger and deals.

Caravan Masters are leaders and master merchants, adaptable to vastly different cultural situations. They come from all walks of life, but all value finding solutions with the right words and a greased palm over bring to bear fire and steel.

Talents
  • Business Network
    Requirements: Caravan
    No Caravan Master worth their salt operates without a network of business associates. Add +3 to your Influence Pool. When you enter a new city or town you may spend 3 Influence to have a business associate be in that town. Define their business and create them with the Character Generation Table. You may add them to your Network.
  • Caravan
    Requirements: None
    Traveling the open steppes, through mountain paths and across harsh deserts for profit takes a special kind of person. When in your caravan, you may spend 1 Influence to find whatever gear you need at any given moment. You may also spend 3 Influence to have a passenger with a useful skill, who can then be added to your Network.
  • Forgery
    Requirements: None
    The caravan routes move through a wide variety of polities. Sometimes it is prudent to have the right documents, and sometimes it is even more prudent to sell fake stock. When you spend 3 Influence, and with the right tools available, you can make a replica of any item that they have knowledge of.
  • Pathfinder
    Requirements: None
    At times, the routes between cities become overrun with bandits looking for easy targets. A practiced Caravan Master can lead their caravan on alternate routes away from danger. You may choose to have the GM reroll any random encounter rolls during any leg of the Journey.
  • Caravansary
    Requirements: Caravan
    Caravansaries are the connective tissue that keep the caravan routes alive and well. Many who call the road home will form strong ties to these oases of civilization. You may automatically make a caravansary friendly, but must be able to put one person who calls it home onto your Network.
  • Culture Shift
    Requirements: Polygot
    Traveling the great trade routes requires an adaptable nature, not just to survive the wilderness, but to survive civilization. The first time you enter a new location you suffer no penalties for cultural differences. All subsequent times you enter that location, you gain a +1D to all social interactions
  • Polygot
    Requirements: None
    Languages come easy to those who are constantly encountering new tongues. Roll a 2D6 and add that many additional languages to your character sheet.
  • Silver Tongued
    Requirements: None
    Ultimately, the goal of a Caravan Master is to acquire wealth, requiring a subtle and clever tongue. You gain a +1D to all social interactions.
  • Master Horseman
    Requirements: Caravan
    A life spent on the road necessitates a mastery of horses. Some Caravan Masters take this to a different level, almost becoming one with their horse. You gain a +2D to all activities regarding horses.
  • Through the Fire
    Requirements: Pathfinder
    Making one's way through the hostile hinterlands of the world sometimes doesn't go so well. Those that surve come out changed. You gain a +1D to all rolls when facing insurmountable odds.
Cultural Synergies

  • Family in Every Town
    Requirements: Rankian Culture
    The Rankian diaspora has left small communities all over the world, particularly along the caravan routes. In any town you enter you may call upon a member of your family so long as add that character to your Network.
  • Mountain Brother
    Requirements: Zeher Culture
    Some stories of the Great Cycle are about allies bound by ties that transcend all differences. In those tales, the great explorer travels with a loyal friend at their side. You have a Zeher companion who stands outside the Network. They are eternally loyal to the character and cannot be turned by the Conspiracy.
