Malins

Malin legend holds that they, as a people, were originally from a land they called Paleki. There they were a soft people living in a soft land of milk an honey. A people used to Paradise on earth, having avoided the machinations of the Jutun, the First Men, or even the Ilan Adam, whom legend says they lived on the edges of Paradise.

This tranquility changed with the great flooding caused by the melting ice that heralded the end of the Jutun. This flooding washed the wards away from the Cin-Seytan Gate, allowing the Jinn to escape their infernal prison. They invaded Paradise, harvesting the Malins, transplanting them to the Great Waste, weeding out the weak and shaping Malin society.

When the Last Prophet (293 3E) came out of the most desolate parts of the wastes to unite the Malin Oymaqs (extended families) against their Jinn overlords, the Malins began to take on the shape they hold today. Over 600 years later, the Malins are still a hard people who adhere to traditions that blend the merciless violence of their time under the Jinn with the religious zeal of the followers of the Last Prophet and the opulence of their neighbor and rival, the Empire.

Names
The naming practices of the Malins is deeply tied to their transplantation to the Great Waste. First names are granted at age 15, reflecting some aspect of their personality. Surnames reflect a conjunction (-ib-) of the father's first name, the name of origin of the individual's Oymaq, and the individual's grandmother (Panah Negar-ib-Deylaq-ib-Inara).

Language
The Malins natively speak Ayun, one of the most common languages in the world. Because of this they also have a passing understanding of the other Duni languages, Hazan, Hanud, and Ranka.

Vocations

A character's Vocation is a Talent that reflects the nature of their cultural background. Social standing, job training, and life experience all determine what capabilities the characters has. Read through the following descriptions and select the Vocation that is most interesting to you.

Secilmis

The court of the Khale Kesis of the Khalifate is one that pulled together powerful tribes and the noble courts of Hikmet to forge a singular political order. Under the direction of the descendents of the Last Prophet five great houses and seventy lesser houses rule the Khalifate through diplomacy, assassination, and war.

  • Ana Kardes
    The Ana Sisterhood is a key social, religious, and political force in the Kalifate. They train their bodies and minds through years of physical and mental conditioning to achieve human perfection. The end result is a society of women who are one part trusted advisor, one part skilled diplomat and one part willing assassin.
    Influence Recovery: +2
  • Erkek
    The Ha'as is the basic social structure of the Malin nobility, which is made up of ten Erkeks. Erkeks are raised to rule the Kalifate and wage war as one of the faithful followers of the Last Prophet.
    Influence Recovery: +1
  • Bashani
    Every municipality in the Khalifate is lead by a Bashani. They function as administrators, judges, and even war leaders when their home is threatened.
    Influence Recovery: +2
  • Odaba
    At the head of each House of the Khalifate, there is an Odaba guiding it's fate. Masters of the great game of politics and death, their machinations can take years or even generations to bear fruit.
    Influence Recovery: +3
Uyanlar

On his deathbed, the Last Prophet decreed that the faithful would be placed in the care of the Uyanlar, or judges. The chief of these judges is the Khale Kesis, elected from the great houses, they serve for life guiding the Khalifate and overseeing the rest of the Uyanlar. In the Khalifate, religion is even more tightly interwoven in society than that of the Empire.

  • Cenaz
    When Jinn are suspected as the cause of unrest, the dreaded Cenaz step forward to bind or destroy them. The only group in the Malin world allowed to learn the Wyrding, they are both despised and needed.
    Influence Recovery: +1
  • Katil Tanri
    The Katil Tanri believe that it is in the manner of their deaths they show their faith. At times assassins and at other times fearless shock troops, they display courage, faith and the righteousness of their path by wading in blood.
    Influence Recovery: +1
  • Muhzar
    The Last Prophet decreed in his first sermon All men deserve the justice of the Twenty Four. The Muhzar are the instrument of that justic, traveling the Khalifate to adjudicated disputes and meet out sentences with a sharp blade.
    Influence Recovery: +2
  • Sual
    The bloodhounds of heresy, the Sual war against the enemies within and without the Khalifate. Typically they travel in packs of five or more, but occassionally lone Sual operate in secrecy on personal crusades.
    Influence Recovery: +1
Isiler

The majority of the population of the Khalifate comes from the Isiler and most stay within the profession of their forefathers. While not as segmented as the Empire, Malin culture tends to have little social movement. It is not unheard of for a Coban to be adopted into a Secilmis house, but few are motivated with that level of ambition.

  • Coban
    Malin culture has long been that of the nomadic herdsman, traveling deserts and savannas searching for water an fodder for their flock. The hard conditions of this life have made the Coban a harsh people unsurprised by conflict.
    Influence Recovery: +1
  • Ciftci
    While images of camel caravans and vast herds of sheep are what come to mind when imagining the common Malin, there is a sedintary aspect of their world as well. Famous for it's fruit, the Malin world is also home to a wide variety of crops, from rice in the southeast to wheat along the river Origem.
    Influence Recovery: +1
  • Denizici
    Though the deep deserts are where the Malin people originally sprang from, over the generations they have taken to roaming the seas with the same level of comfort. Malin pirates patrol the warm seas alongside Malin merchants who trade with friend and foe alike.
    Influence Recovery: +1
  • Tuccar
    The Tuccar are at once how Malin society stays connected across vast distances and how the spread of the words of the Last Prophet has continued without a conqueror's sword. Some stay in one city, plying their trade, and others travel about the world, but all Tuccar act as reliable sources of information.
    Influence Recovery: +2
Alinan

Malin society has always been built on the backs of those who have no choice but to serve. Taken from all over the world, Alinan are raised knowing that they are property to be disposed of as their master pleases. No more, no less.

  • Kole
    It is somewhat surprising that Malins would arm the Alinan and train them to be elite soldiers. However the conditioning is so absolute that only twice in the long history of their use has there been an uprising of Koles.
    Influence Recovery: +0
  • Elnek
    Castrated at a young age, the Elnek are often the only individuals trusted among the Uyanlar. There training is specialized and varied, covering everything from grooming and healing to poisons and diplomacy.
    Influence Recovery: +0
