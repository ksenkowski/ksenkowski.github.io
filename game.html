Conspiracy of Shadows Simple RPG

I used to design and publish role-playing games. For about ten years I have experimented and designed dozens of games, fragments of games, and a few fully realized, published games, and throughout that entire time I was never truly happy. Since I stopped working on games for publication I find myself much happier focusing my creative energies elsewhere.

The fact that I stopped designing games for publication doesn't mean that I have stopped designing and playing games. The following outline encompasses the basic rules that I am using with my friend Nathan to play a Conspiracy of Shadows game. It is an amalgam of game ideas, some my own, some from designers I respect and admire.

The entire goal of this page is to work as a reference for Nathan and I as we stumble through a little game. It is a hack, there are holes, it will change a lot as we play, and some areas are clearly more worked out than others. To play it all you need is a big bunch of six sided dice, several different colored tokens, and the cards linked below.

Characters

Characters are created through a simple question and answer process. This creates the character's list of Descriptors and Relationships as well as sets the groundwork for the hard stats. It is essentially a conversation between the player and the GM to figure out what exactly the game will be about. Below I have listed the questions as well as a summary of what Nathan answered for his character.

Basic Mechanics

The basic mechanic has two parts. One is a resource management mechanic tied to the character's three main resources and the other is the actual dice. When a character falls into opposition with the Game Master, meaning the Game Master has decided to say no to the player's narration of events, the GM first determines what kind of Challenge the character must overcome.

Challenges fall into six categories. These categories dictate how many successes the player must roll as well as what the cost is to the appropriate resource. Vitality is required to engage in any physical activity, Influence for any social activity, and Willpower can be used for any physical, mental or sorcerous activity. The following chart illustrates the costs and requirements per Challenge.

Challenge Difficulty Successes Cost Harm Duration
Tough 2 1 1 Scene
Challenging 3 2 2 Scenes
Formidable 4 3 3 Scenes
Heroic 5 4 4 Scenes
Super Heroic 6 5 5 Scenes
Supernatural 7 6 6 Scenes

To resolve a Challenge the player rolls a number of six sided dice equal to the skill appropriate to the Challenge plus an additional die for each appropriate Descriptor piece of Gear.

Should a character fail, the GM has two options they can pursue. They can either deny the character what they want (boring), give the character what they want and assign them Harm or give the character what they want and add a complication to their plan.

Harm is essentially a condition that applies a -1D to a specific set of circumstances for a specific set of time. The time is dictated as seen in the Challenge chart above and the circumstances are based on the context of the situation as interpreted by the GM.

Complications are narrative elements that shift the story in another direction as defined by the Game Master. The typical flow of the game is for the player(s) to narrate what they are doing and for the GM to interact. Complications are the opportunity for the Game Master to move the game in a different direction.

Duels

This is the structure of a Duel. Duels go on until one character is incapacitated or surrenders. Will/Taint can be used to augment their character's Vitality Pool. Any Taint that appears counts only once towards the character's Taint progression and is resolved after the Duel is completed.

Duels are to incapacitation. A characters is incapacitated if they have lost all of their Vitality Pool immediately after card resolution. At this time, the opponent can choose to dispatch the character or let them live.

After a Duel the GM determines the physical fallout of the conflict. Use the following chart as a guideline to determine the type of Harm a character suffers.

Wound Type Recommended Harm Duration
Superficial -1D to Physical Actions 1 Scene Per Wound
Deep -2D to Physical Actions 1 Week Per Wound
Massive -1D to Physical Actions Permanent

The initial state of a Duel is dictated by the narration of the Scene leading up to it. Whichever character is the aggressor (initiating the violence), that character has the Tempo. Having the Tempo dictates what cards you can play.

  1. Place the Tempo card in front of the player whose character has the Tempo.
  2. Each player takes cards from his Duel Deck into their hand equal to their Warrior Skill. This is their plan of action. At any point a player may spend 1 Vitality or Willpower/Taint Point to swap a card from their hand with a card from their deck.
  3. Both player simultaneously places a token on his Guard card indicating his starting Guard.
  4. The player with the Tempo lays their first card(s) and pays the Vitality cost. The Player must declare what part of the body of their opponent they are targeting.
  5. The defender answers the attack with:
    • Playing a Defense card, declaring they are done, and pays the Vitality cost.
    • Playing a Defense card, an Attack card and pays the Vitality cost. The Player must declare what part of the body of their opponent they are targeting.
    • Playing an Attack card and pays the Vitality cost.
  6. The player with Tempo answers the defender's response with:
    • When faced with a Defense card, declares that they are done and resolves the cards.
    • When faced with a combo of a Defense and Attack card either:
      • Declares that they are done and resolves the cards.
      • Plays a Defense card.
  7. The Exchange is now resolved. If at any time during an Exchange on player is successful (such as the initial attack), the follow up cards are ignored and brought back into each player's hand.
    1. The player with the Tempo card makes their draw as indicated by their card and their weapon.
    2. The defender makes their draw as indicated by their card and their weapon.
      • If the defender wins but did not play an attack card as a follow up, the Tempo stays with the attacker and continue the Exchange.
      • If the defender wins and did play an attack card as a follow up, they gain the Tempo card and draw for their attack card. If the initial attacker in the Exchange played a defense card, resolve the cards.
    To resolve the cards, the player with the most successes wins the resolution. The attacker then compares their net number of successes (the attacker's successes minus the defenders's successes) against their opponent's armor value for that location and subtracts that number from their successes. If there are any successes remaining, they are compared against the Harm chart and the effect is applied.
  8. If all of the characters are still alive, capable, and still have cards in their hand, return to step five. Continue this cycle until either a character is dead, incapacitated, one player has no more cards to play or a player disengages.
  9. Once a character either disengages or one player runs out of cards from their hand, the Exchange is over. Each character recovers Vitality equal to their recovery rate. If a character disengages while having the Tempo card, they now lose it to their opponent. Otherwise, the character with the Tempo card retains it for the next Exchange, starting at Step 1 above.

Measure & The Lines

Measure is the relative distance between two opponents that dictates what cards can be played and what impact they have. The Measure card is placed between the two players with a coin placed upon the starting Measure. As Measure changes through the movement of the characters, move the coin. In the case of multiple opponents, each opponent has it's own Measure card to show their relative positioning.

Only Guard Change and movement cards can be used Out of Measure. Using Disengage here ends the Duel via flight.

Only the Passing Attack action can be played from Wide Measure in Tempo. All other attacks grant Tempo to the opponent.

All actions save Grapple and Toss are possible in Narrow Measure.

All actions save Girata and Passing Attack are possible in the Clinch.

The Lines

There are two lines in which you may attack and defend, the Inside and Outside line. Several action cards are marked with either the Inside or Outside line. Specific Guards work better against different lines of attack.

The Guards

Guard Descriptions

Second +2D Parry against all attacks from the Outside Line.
Third +1D to Strikes and Thrust cards.
